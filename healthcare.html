i) Efficient Waste Collection and Distribution Breadth-First Search (BFS) Map urban areas to identify optimal waste collection points and ensure efficient routes for waste collection trucks.
  • SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities – Promotes effective waste management in urban areas.
  • SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production – Encourages sustainable waste handling and reduction practices.
ii) Recycling Facility Optimization Dijkstra’s Algorithm Optimize transportation of recyclable materials to the nearest processing facilities to reduce costs and energy consumption.
  • SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production – Supports the creation of a circular economy.
  • SDG 13: Climate Action – Reduces carbon footprint by minimizing transportation inefficiencies.
iii) Hazardous Waste Prioritization Heap Sort Rank and prioritize hazardous waste based on risk levels for proper and timely disposal.
  • SDG 3: Good Health and Well-Being – Minimizes health risks from hazardous waste.
  • SDG 15: Life on Land – Ensures safe disposal to protect ecosystems and biodiversity.
iv) Waste Tracking and Monitoring HashMaps Search and monitor waste generation patterns by analyzing records for trends, anomalies, or inefficiencies.
  • SDG 16: Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions – Encourages transparency and accountability in waste management systems.
  • SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure – Promotes smart technologies for efficient waste tracking.
